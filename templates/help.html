When people get stuck

The most common confusion points, explained simply.

Do I need to understand the score deeply?

No. The score is mainly a shortcut that tells you whether the overall position is strong, moderate, or still early-stage.

What should I open after an audit?

Open the Growth Plan first. That is where the findings become useful action.

Should I create a brief or a draft first?

Use a brief when you want structure and approval first. Use a draft when the topic is already clear and you want speed.

When should I re-run an audit?

After important new pages are published, after major revisions, or when you want to check whether visibility is improving.

3 practical guides

Keep it simple and repeatable.

I am new. Where do I start?

  1. Go to Clients.
  2. Add the business.
  3. Open the client workspace.
  4. Run an audit.
  5. Open the Growth Plan.

I have a report. What next?

  1. Read the top priority actions.
  2. Choose the first 1–3 actions only.
  3. Create a brief or draft.
  4. Publish improvements.
  5. Re-run the audit later.

How do I explain this to a client?

  1. Start with the overall score.
  2. Explain what it means in plain English.
  3. Show the top 2–3 actions.
  4. Do not overwhelm them with every detail.
  5. End with the next 30-day plan.
