Think of this platform as a tool that shows whether your business is easy to find in AI answers and modern search, then tells you what to improve next. Most users only need to follow the workflow below.

Glossary

Simple meanings without technical jargon.

AEO

Answer Engine Optimization. This means helping your business show up better in AI answers and modern search experiences.

Visibility

Whether your business is being found when customers search or ask AI tools questions.

Report

A scan of your website that checks how visible and competitive your business is.

Growth Plan

A simplified action plan based on your latest report. This is usually the best page to open after a report is ready.

Content Brief

A structured outline for a page or article your team should create.

Content Draft

A first draft generated from your brief and business context.

Competitor Gap

A situation where competitors are being shown, but your business is missing.

Schema

Technical website labels that help search engines and AI systems understand your business more clearly.

Opportunity Level

A simple signal showing how much upside there is if you improve your website and content.

Content Queue

The workflow area where briefs and drafts are stored so they can move toward production and publishing.

Guides

Practical walkthroughs for common tasks.

Guide 1: I am new. Where do I start?

  1. Go to Clients.
  2. Add your business name and website.
  3. Open the client workspace.
  4. Click Run New Audit.
  5. Open Growth Plan after the report is ready.

Guide 2: I already have a report. What do I do next?

  1. Open the client’s Growth Plan.
  2. Read the Priority Actions.
  3. Choose the most urgent issue first.
  4. Generate a Content Brief or Content Draft.
  5. Save work into the Content Queue.

Guide 3: How do I explain this to a client or boss?

  1. Open the Presentation page.
  2. Show the overall score and opportunity level first.
  3. Explain the top 2–3 priority actions only.
  4. Use the Growth Plan to show what should happen next.
  5. Export or print to PDF if needed.

Guide 4: What if I do not understand the scores?

Do not worry too much about the numbers themselves. Focus on what they are trying to tell you:

  • Low visibility means your business is not appearing often enough.
  • Low content score means your website may not answer enough customer questions.
  • Low schema score means your site may not be clear enough for machines to interpret.
  • Opportunity level means how much upside there may be if you improve what is missing.

Guide 5: When should I create a brief instead of a full draft?

  • Create a brief when you want the structure and plan first.
  • Create a draft when the topic is already clear and you want content faster.
  • Use briefs first when the client needs approval before writing starts.

Guide 6: When should I re-run an audit?

  • After important new pages are published.
  • After major edits to key service pages or FAQs.
  • When you want to check whether visibility is improving over time.
